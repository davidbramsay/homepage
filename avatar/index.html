Avatar Project

Virtual Pets vs. Virtual Selves for Exercise Motivation



Virtual pet games- in which a user regularly cares for a small creature- have been effectively used as health behavior change agents with children. Research using virtual selves (avatars) have also provided promising results.

We outfitted two groups (N=20) of college-age students with fitness trackers that controlled either (1) the health and happiness of a virtual pet or (2) the weight and happiness of a virtual avatar, and compared the effects on their exercise patterns over the course of two weeks. We also measured participant narcissism, empathy, and self-image to compare psychographic data against exercise motivation and pet/avatar attachment.

A version of the avatar-based version of the webapp is hosted live at minime.davidbramsay.com.
